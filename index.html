Against dog cruelty

Animal cruelty is an upsetting problem that is more common than any of us would like to believe. Domestic animals rely on humans for shelter, food, water, and kindness. Too many of them receive the opposite. A person beats, stabs, burns, or otherwise harms a dog purposely. It is not usually possible to tell from an animal's behavior whether he is being abused. Some dogs are aggressive, fearful, or timid for reasons other than abuse. Look for clues in the dog's environment and on the dog himself, such as open wounds(dogs will often have open wounds that appear to be untreated),weakness or inability to stand and other clues. A person hitting, kicking, or otherwise physically harming a dog,if you see someone like this actively harming a dog, do not confront the person because you may put yourself at risk of injury, as well.People who abuse animals are more likely to be violent toward other people.
